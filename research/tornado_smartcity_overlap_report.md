## LAYER 2: THE AGENDA FRAMEWORK -- HOW IT'S REALLY NAMED

The public discourse misses the overlap because the branding has shifted multiple times. Here is the lineage:

```
Agenda 21 (1992, Rio Earth Summit)
  --> Local Agenda 21 (ICLEI, 1991-2003)
    --> "Sustainable Development" / "Livability" / "Smart Growth" (2000s rebranding)
      --> Agenda 2030 / 17 SDGs (2015, UN)
        --> "Smart Cities" / "15-Minute City" / "Resilient City" (2015-present)
          --> Freedom Cities (2023-present, domestic rebranding)
```

**Key fact:** ICLEI (International Council for Local Environmental Initiatives) rebranded to **"ICLEI -- Local Governments for Sustainability"** in 2003 specifically to be, in their own internal communications, "less visible." The mission did not change. The US never widely adopted the "Local Agenda 21" label -- instead these plans were pushed locally under names like:

- "2030 Comprehensive Plan" (Kankakee County's exact plan title)
- "Sustainable Communities" initiative
- "Greenest Region Compact"
- "Climate Action Plan"
- "NWI 2050+" (Northwest Indiana Regional Planning Commission)
- "Voluntary Local Review" (VLR)
- "Global Covenant of Mayors"
- "C40 Cities"

Every one of these is a downstream implementation vehicle for the same UN SDG framework.

## LAYER 5: THE 15-MINUTE CITY VECTOR

The "15-minute city" concept -- all services within 15-minute walk/bike -- is the urban design implementation arm of SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities). In the tornado region:

- **Chicago** (C40 member, tornado hail zone) passed its first-ever **proactive rezoning** in 2024 for the Western Avenue Corridor -- the B3-3 mixed-use density model used in 15-minute city frameworks. Effective April 2026: ground-floor residential allowed in commercial zones citywide.
- **Chicago** announced a **Comprehensive Climate Action Plan** (May 2025) targeting net-zero by 2050, 80% GHG reduction from 2020 levels.
- **Cleveland** (adjacent region) is the **first North American city** with a formal 15-minute city government framework -- Form-Based Code pilot in 3 neighborhoods as of 2024.
- **NIRPC NWI 2050+** explicitly supports "compact development," "transit-oriented growth," and mixed-use density -- the physical implementation of the 15-minute city model -- across the tornado corridor counties.

## LAYER 6: DISASTER GENTRIFICATION -- THE DOCUMENTED PLAYBOOK

This is not theoretical. It is a documented, active practice:

**St. Louis, MO -- EF3 tornado, May 2025**
- Institutional investors were calling victims the morning after the storm
- 63% of renters and 49% of homeowners in the most damaged neighborhoods were uninsured
- Mayor had to pause tax sales to stop land grabs
- 5 community-led planning committees launched Sept 2025 to rebuild with smart building codes and walkable urban design

**Buncombe County, NC -- Hurricane Helene, 2025**
- 205-page Helene Recovery Plan approved Nov 2025
- 114 projects across 7 municipalities
- Explicit focus: affordable housing outside floodplains, "resiliency hubs," smart water systems
- Top listed priority: "Preserving flood-prone property from development" (i.e., acquisition)

**North Sioux City, SD -- Floods, 2024**
- City spent $3.98M acquiring 16 flood-damaged lots for "land remediation and reconstruction"

**National pattern (WIRED/Grist, Sept 2025)**
- North Carolina: Residents received constant investor texts for weeks after Helene
- Pattern documented across every major 2025 disaster zone

**Federal amplifier: EDA FY2025 Disaster Supplemental Program**
- $1.45 billion available for disaster-declared areas
- Eligible uses: capacity building, strategic planning, construction, economic recovery, "Industry Transformation"
- This is the federal pipeline that converts disaster zones into transformation zones
